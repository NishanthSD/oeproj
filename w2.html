Squat Workout Tutorial

Squats are a fundamental exercise that works your entire lower body, including your quads, glutes, and hamstrings. They can be done with or without weights, and they're a great way to build strength and improve your overall fitness.

Here's how to do a proper squat:

Proper Squat Form

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointed slightly outward.
  2. Lower your body as if you're going to sit in a chair, keeping your back straight and your core engaged.
  3. Make sure your knees don't track inwards, and keep your heels flat on the ground.
  4. Descend until your thighs are parallel to the ground (or as low as you can comfortably go).
  5. Push through your heels to return to the starting position.
  • Focus on keeping your core engaged throughout the movement.
  • Don't let your knees cave in. They should track in line with your toes.
  • Keep your chest lifted and avoid hunching your back.
  • Breathe out as you exert yourself on the way up.
  • Start with bodyweight squats and gradually add weight as you get stronger.

  • Increased lower body strength and power.
  • Improved core stability and balance.
  • Enhanced bone density.
  • Boosted metabolism.
  • Functional movement for everyday activities.
